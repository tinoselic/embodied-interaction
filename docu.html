1. Gesture Assignment
Posted 07.02.23

In this group work Guus, Simon, Yan and I had to think about a gesture that we use in our everyday life. This gesture should then be used for a concept of a gestural interface. We chose to work with the hand gesture used to improve hearing when trying to listen closely. We then thought about a narrative and came up with a story about an encaptured snake that is revealed by doing the gesture. On a wall there is a projection of a grey and noisey video, the audio is distorted and quiet too. When doing the hand gesture, a spotlight is casted onto the caged snake and audio and video become saturated and clear.
2. Final Project: Proposal
Posted 07.03.23


IDEA
A starry night sky is projected onto the ceiling of a dark space. Depending on the positions of the visitors in the space, star constellations are generated. If a visitor stands in the center of the space auroras will appear. This person can control the appearance of auroras by moving their arms and hands.

CONCEPT
In touchdesigner I create a black canvas with small glowing white circles that appear on random positions and resemble stars. I track the positions of multiple people (Lidar?) and capture the data in certain time intervals. I then convert this data into specific star constillations that appear for a limited time by connecting certain circles/stars with white/grey lines.
The appearance of auroras could be realised with a particle system of white, green and red/purple colored shapes (lines, circles, sprites?). This effect is put ontop of the night sky as a partly transparent layer. The visitor positioned closest to the center of the space is regognized to make the auroras appear. This persons wrists are tracked and depending on the wrists movement the aurora motion will be affected (translate, scale, distort, displace). Alternatively, the aurora effect could be simulated by using light that is casted into the space onto the visitors. The color of the light would change with the movement of the controler's wrist.
